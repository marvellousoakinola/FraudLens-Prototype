FraudLens is a cybersecurity platform I conceived and developed to address the increasing threat of phishing attacks, malicious links, and digital fraud. The project began as an idea to create a tool that not only detects threats but also explains them in a way that everyday users can understand.

I led the design and development of the platform from the ground up, defining its core concept, architecture, and user experience. I built the entire frontend application using React and TypeScript, developing all key interfaces including the scanning system, results analysis page, authentication flow, and user dashboard. My focus was on creating a system that is both technically effective and accessible, allowing users to quickly assess risk and understand the reasoning behind each result.

Beyond the frontend, I took responsibility for ensuring the overall system functioned as a complete, integrated product. I worked directly with backend APIs, handling integration, testing, and debugging to ensure reliable communication between components. During development, I identified and resolved critical issues affecting system stability, including server runtime failures, broken API connections, and inconsistencies in database behavior. Where necessary, I modified backend logic and configurations to restore functionality and improve performance.

Although I sought assistance in specific areas, I maintained ownership of the project’s direction and execution, making key decisions across both frontend and backend aspects. This required me to think beyond individual components and focus on building a cohesive, working system.

FraudLens reflects my ability to take an idea from concept to implementation, adapt to challenges, and deliver a functional solution to a real-world problem. My long-term goal is to continue developing impactful cybersecurity tools that improve digital safety and awareness, particularly in underserved communities.
